DUAL AXIS SOLAR TRACKER

  • Category: Hardware
  • Programming Language: Arduino
  • Topic: Digital Logic and Design Analysis

Introduction

  • Most solar cells run at efficiency of 18-20%
  • This is because they are in a fixed position at around 45 degrees
  • The best way to increase efficiency is to have it track the sun.
  • Sun tracking solar panels produce 30% more energy than fixed panels.

Dual Axis

Our tracker is a dual axis tracker, meaning it tracks in both X and Y. To put it into even more simple terms, it goes left, right, up, and down. This means once you have your tracker set up you will never need to change or adjust anything, since anywhere the sun moves your tracker will follow. This method gives the best results for power generation.

Working

The program works by comparing the resistance of the four sensors and moving the servos. The servos then move the panel toward the LDR which receives more sunlight (least resistance).

Construction

  • Base and Arm made with dual layered cardboard
  • Modular design for easy assembly and disassembly
  • 1 Servo for vertical movement other for horizontal movement
  • LDRs attached to 4 corners of a long cross shape structure (+)
  • Arduino Shield for easy installation of servo to Arduino
  • JST connectors to attach LDRs to the Arduino board

Program code algorithm

  • Set limit of vertical and horizontal servos
  • Compare the LDR potential values
  • Compare TL,TR and BL,BR to move vertical servo
  • Compare TL,BL and TR,BR to move horizontal servo
